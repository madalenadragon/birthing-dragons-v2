Your Good Girl Spirituality Is Keeping You Broke

Trigger warning. You were taught that being spiritual means having no ego, no desires, no ambition. That is not spirituality. That is a cage. And it was designed specifically to keep you powerless.

Let me say something that might sting, and that might also set you free.

You have been told that to be spiritual, you must be humble. Patient. Of service. Without ego. You have been told to transcend your materialism, to just be here now, to surrender your ambition because it is your ego's attachment. You have been told that if you were truly evolved, you would not care about money or any material things.

What a profound amount of pseudo-spiritual nonsense.

The Cage Dressed as Enlightenment

This is the spiritual bypass of money. It is the purity culture of poverty dressed up as enlightenment. And it is a cage, designed specifically to keep you ungrounded, under-resourced, confused and powerless.

They taught you that being spiritual means having no ego, no desires, no drive, no boundaries. And many deeply sincere, genuinely seeking women believed them. Not because they were naive, but because the message came wrapped in the language of evolution, of love, of transcendence. It sounded holy. It felt like growth. But underneath it was a wound, and underneath the wound was a system that benefits enormously from spiritual women staying broke, dependent and small.

When you deny your desires, your nervous system contracts and your energetic field shrinks. When you deny your ego, your power and your drive, your capacity to hold wealth, build something meaningful and create real impact either significantly diminishes or disappears entirely. Because you are operating from a wound dressed as a spiritual virtue. And that is not spirituality. That is self-sabotage with better language.

Denying your desires for wealth, freedom, luxury, power, love and impact is not humility. It is violence against your own expansion. It is the internalized oppression of a system that wants you small, powerless, struggling and poor.

Your Desires Are Not Impure

Denying your desires for wealth, freedom, luxury, power, love, impact, or whatever else you genuinely want within reason, integrity and respect, is not humility. It is violence against your own expansion. It is the internalised oppression of a system that wants you small, powerless, struggling and poor. And your soul, underneath all the conditioning, has been screaming at you to wake up to this.

Your ambition is not impure. Your desire for wealth is not unspiritual. There is nothing inherently wrong or bad about your ego. Your refusal to play small is not narcissistic or selfish. It is your power begging to be honoured. And the pseudo-spiritual cage was built specifically to convince you otherwise, to keep you from realising just how much capacity for leadership, wealth and impact you actually carry.

What Embodied Spiritual Leadership Actually Looks Like

Embodied spiritual leaders do not reject wealth. They command it. They wield it as a tool for transformation, for impact, for building the new paradigm that everyone else keeps talking about but very few are actually funding.

Your abundance is not selfish. It is not unspiritual. It literally fuels the very movement you are here to lead. You cannot create change from a position of financial scarcity and dependence. You cannot build the new earth while rejecting its resources. The leaders who will actually shift things are not the ones who transcended their desires for power and wealth. They are the ones who learned to hold power and wealth with integrity, and deployed them in service of something larger than themselves.

Embodied spiritual leaders do not reject wealth. They command it. They wield it as a tool for transformation and impact and to build the new paradigm everyone else keeps talking about.

It Is Time to Shatter the Cage

The pseudo-spiritual cage is real. The conditioning is deep. And dismantling it is not a one-time decision. It is a practice of returning, again and again, to your own sovereign knowing. Of choosing your desires over the conditioning. Of refusing to perform poverty consciousness as a spiritual credential.

You were not born to be small and humble in the way that system taught you. You were born to lead. To build. To hold power with integrity and wisdom. To create wealth and deploy it in alignment with your values. That is not the opposite of spirituality. That is spirituality doing its full work in the world, through you, through your embodied life, through the very tangible things you build and leave behind.

The cage was always a lie. You always knew it. Now it is time to act like it.

With Love,
Madalena Dragon
