Spirituality and Money: The Belief That Is Keeping You Poor

Spiritual people believing that money is the root of all evil is the actual root of all evil.

There is a belief that runs deep in spiritual communities, sometimes spoken openly, sometimes just quietly held as a background assumption that colours every financial decision, every sales conversation, every moment of pricing a service or receiving payment for sacred work.

The belief that money and spirituality cannot coexist. That true spiritual people are not supposed to want wealth. That charging abundantly for healing work is somehow a corruption of it. That the more enlightened you are, the less you should care about money, and the less money you should need.

It sounds humble. It sounds pure. And it is one of the most damaging beliefs in the entire spiritual space.

Where This Belief Actually Comes From

This is not ancient wisdom. This is not a universal spiritual truth. This is centuries of patriarchal religious conditioning that taught people, and women especially, that suffering was holy, that desiring material things was sinful, that the truly devoted renounced earthly wealth and proved their righteousness through lack.

That conditioning did not disappear when people left organised religion and entered New Age spirituality. It followed them. It simply dressed itself in different language. Instead of "the meek shall inherit the earth," it became "money is just energy" paired with a simultaneous, unexamined discomfort with actually having any. Instead of vows of poverty, it became chronically undercharging, overgiving, and calling it generosity.

The wound changed its costume. The wound remained.

What It Is Actually Costing You

When a spiritual person believes, at some level, consciously or not, that wealth conflicts with their values, here is what tends to happen:

  • They undercharge for their work, because asking for full value feels greedy or unsafe
  • They overgive, sessions that run over, extras added for free, boundaries that collapse, because they are trying to earn their right to receive
  • They avoid selling, because selling feels like manipulation rather than an invitation into transformation
  • They attract clients who are not truly invested, because low prices signal low stakes
  • They burn out, because they are giving more than they are receiving, not as a spiritual practice, but as a pattern rooted in unworthiness

The world does not need more spiritually gifted, deeply devoted healers and leaders who are broke. It needs them resourced, sustainable, and able to do their work at the scale their gifts deserve.

Spiritual people believing that money is the root of all evil is the actual root of all evil. A healer without a thriving business is just a gifted person the world cannot fully access.

The Sales Ick Is a Nervous System Response

If you feel a visceral discomfort around selling, the ick, the freeze, the way your whole system recoils at the idea of making an offer, naming your price, following up, I want to offer you a reframe that may be more useful than another mindset shift.

That ick is not a moral compass. It is a nervous system response.

It might look like freeze: you don't post, you don't pitch, you go quiet right when visibility is most needed. It might look like fawn: you overprice downward, you add bonuses, you make yourself smaller so the other person feels comfortable. It might look like flight: you rebrand again, you pivot the offer, you start over instead of selling what you have. It might look like fight: you judge other people who sell confidently and call them manipulative.

Your body learned, somewhere, that it was not safe to ask for what you are worth. That receiving abundance would cost you something, connection, approval, your spiritual identity, your sense of being good. And now it is protecting you from that imagined danger every time you try to sell.

The work is not to push through the ick with more willpower. The work is to regulate the nervous system underneath it. To make it genuinely safe, in your body, not just in your mind, to receive.

Making Money IS Spiritual

Selling is not manipulation. A sales conversation, done with integrity and genuine care, is an invitation into transformation. It is leadership. It is clarity. It is someone saying: I see what is possible for you, here is the path, here is the door, and I trust you enough to show you all of it without hiding the price.

Money is not the opposite of spirituality. Money is energy, amplified. It amplifies what is already there. In the hands of someone with genuine gifts, genuine devotion, and a real mission in the world, money becomes impact. It becomes reach. It becomes the ability to create the containers, the spaces, the resources that allow more people to be transformed.

The most spiritual thing a healer, a teacher, a leader can do is build a thriving business. Not in spite of their values, but as the fullest expression of them.

Making money is spiritual. Building a thriving business with your sacred gifts is one of the most powerful acts of devotion you can offer the world.

Money and Nervous System Safety

The deepest money work is not about mindset. It is not about affirmations or abundance journaling or clearing your money blocks, though all of those can be useful tools. The deepest money work is about making it genuinely safe, at the level of your nervous system, to receive.

To receive money. To receive recognition. To receive clients who are fully invested. To receive the acknowledgement that your work is worth what you say it is worth, and not feel the immediate impulse to give it all back, shrink the price, add more value to justify it, or sabotage the relationship before it can fully begin.

This is the integration. Not spiritual OR wealthy. Not devoted OR successful. Both. Fully. Without apology. As the living proof that a woman can be genuinely sacred and genuinely resourced, and that one deepens the other.

The world needs that proof right now. And you are the one who gets to provide it.
