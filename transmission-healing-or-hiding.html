Are You Healing or Hiding?

Healing is sacred work. And there is a version of it that has become a very sophisticated form of hiding. The two can look identical from the outside. Only you know which one you are really in.

Madalena Dragon Embodiment & Spirituality 6 min read

I want to say something that might be uncomfortable, because I spent years living it and nobody said it to me directly.

You can use healing as a reason to never fully show up.

You can spend years in therapy, in breathwork, in ceremony, in retreat, going deeper and deeper into your story, your wounds, your patterns, and use all of that as evidence that you are not ready. Not healed enough. Not safe enough. Not whole enough to build the thing, launch the thing, be seen in the way your soul is calling you toward.

Healing can be an act of courage and expansion. It can also be an extremely sophisticated hiding place.

And the hardest part is that both look exactly the same from the outside.

What Real Healing Looks Like

Real healing increases your capacity to show up in your life. It makes you more available, not less. More embodied, more present, more willing to take up space, more capable of being seen.

When you are genuinely healing, there is movement. Things shift. Patterns change. Your relationship with risk, visibility, desire, and intimacy evolves. You are different this year than last year in ways you can point to.

Real healing builds a life. It does not just build an understanding of why the life you want feels out of reach.

What Healing as Hiding Looks Like

Healing as hiding looks like this.

You have been in healing modalities for years, and the same core patterns are still running your life. You can describe your wounds with extraordinary precision but the behaviour has not changed. You know exactly which childhood experience is responsible for your visibility block, and you still do not post the content.

You keep finding new layers to heal before you can move forward. Every time you get close to a breakthrough, a new wound surfaces that needs attention first. The healing never quite completes, because completion would require showing up, and showing up feels terrifying.

You are in deep devotion to your healing practice and simultaneously your mission, your business, your income, your creative expression are all still waiting.

The Question Nobody Asks

Here is the question I wish someone had asked me years earlier: what would you do differently if you were fully healed?

Go on. Actually answer it. What would change? What would you start? What would you say? What would you charge? What would you stop tolerating?

Now ask yourself: what is actually stopping you from doing that right now?

Because here is what I have learned working with hundreds of women through deep transformation: the healing does not come before the action. For most of us, the healing happens through the action.

You do not get healed and then visible. You get visible and the being seen heals something that years of inner work could not reach.

You do not get healed and then charge what you are worth. You charge what you are worth and the receiving of it rewires something at a cellular level.

You do not get healed and then build the thing. You build the thing and discover capacities inside yourself that no healing modality could have handed you.

The Permission You Have Been Waiting For

You do not need to be fully healed to begin.

No one is. No one ever will be. The humans doing the most powerful, most visible, most impactful work in the world are not more healed than you. They are just more willing to move with the wound still present.

Bring your wound with you. Let it come along for the ride. It will heal faster in motion than it ever will in stillness.

The work you are here to do is not waiting for your healing to be complete. It is part of your healing.

The question is not: am I healed enough? The question is: am I willing to find out what I am capable of?
