The Spirituality We Were Never Taught

Mainstream spirituality has glorified one path for centuries. It is time to reclaim the other.

For centuries, the mainstream path to spiritual enlightenment has looked like this: ascend. Renounce. Leave the body behind. Seek higher states of consciousness. Transcend desire, transcend pleasure, transcend the chaos of ordinary human life. Reach for the divine by leaving the human.

This path was labelled holy. Pure. Enlightened. And for many, it provided something real, discipline, stillness, liberation from suffering. I do not diminish that.

But it was only half the story. And the half that was missing was the feminine.

Two Paths, One Truth

The masculine path of spirituality is the path of transcendence and ascension. It moves upward. It seeks the divine through logic, stillness, and silence, by rising above the material, detaching from the earthly, reaching for something beyond the human experience.

The feminine path of spirituality is the path of embodiment and descension. It moves downward. It seeks the divine through emotion, feeling, and sensation, by descending fully into the body, into the earth, into the rawness of human experience itself.

The masculine seeks enlightenment beyond form. The feminine awakens through form.

One is not better than the other. But for a very long time, we were only taught one, and told the other was a deviation, a temptation, something to transcend on the way to becoming truly spiritual.

What We Lost When We Abandoned the Feminine

When mainstream spirituality glorified transcendence, it quietly, systematically, dismissed everything the feminine held sacred: the body, emotions, pleasure, sensation, the earth, cycles, mystery, chaos, the deep wisdom of the womb.

We learned to abandon our humanness in the name of God. To treat our emotions as obstacles rather than gateways. To distrust the body rather than listen to it. To call earthly desires impure rather than sacred.

We were taught, in essence, that the path to the divine required us to stop being fully human. And so we split ourselves in two, and called it enlightenment.

The Feminine Doesn't Transcend, She Descends

The feminine path does not seek to escape life. She becomes more alive within it.

She descends into the body, not away from it. Into the heart, not away from it. Into emotion, into sensation, into the earth. She does not run from chaos, she moves through it until it becomes wisdom. She does not detach from her humanness, she makes it holy.

Enlightenment, from the feminine perspective, is not about transcending your humanity to reach your divinity in spirit. It is about descending into your humanity and bringing your divinity into your body. It is not about becoming less human. It is about becoming more fully, more sacredly, more unapologetically alive.

You are not just here to transcend yourself to reach the Divine. You are here to remember that you ARE the Divine, embodied.

Reclaiming Both Paths

This is not an argument against the masculine path. It is a remembering of the one we lost.

True spirituality, in its fullest expression, holds both. It honours the silence and the song. The stillness and the ecstatic movement. The transcendence and the embodiment. The ascension and the descent.

You are both. You always were. You are the temple and the prayer. You are the one who reaches for the stars and the one who has her feet firmly planted in the earth.

And it is time to stop asking permission to be whole.

With Love,
Madalena Dragon
