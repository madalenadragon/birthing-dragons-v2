Power, Collective Trauma and the Leadership Awakening

We are living through something that deserves to be felt fully, not transcended away. The world is in pain. And we are being called into our humanness and our leadership more than ever.

I want to speak to something that I see happening in the spiritual community, and in myself, that I believe deserves more honesty than it is getting.

The world is in pain. Real, visible, undeniable pain. The most horrible things are happening in front of our eyes. And in the middle of all of it, many of us who are spiritually awake are here, trying to hold our frequency, meditating, doing our inner work, processing our own triggers and patterns. Which is absolutely needed. And also, there is a question we need to sit with honestly: when does the practice of maintaining our inner peace become a sophisticated form of looking away?

The Spiritual Bypass of Collective Pain

There is a particular kind of spiritual bypass that has become very common, and I understand it deeply because I have felt its pull myself. It is the impulse to rise above what is happening and call it a simulation. To find the higher perspective, the one where this is all a necessary part of the great awakening, where every atrocity is somehow serving the evolution of consciousness, that makes the suffering feel more manageable. Less personal. Easier to hold.

I understand why we reach for this. Feeling the full weight of collective pain is almost unbearable for highly sensitive people. It floods the nervous system. It activates grief and rage and helplessness in proportions that feel impossible to integrate. The higher perspective genuinely helps. Seeing the larger arc genuinely matters.

And still. What we sometimes call transcendence is sometimes just a more enlightened-sounding word for looking away.

Dissociating is not spiritual evolution. It is the wound talking, the part of us that learned it was safer to rise above than to stand in the fire of what is actually happening.

What Collective Trauma Does to Us

Collective trauma does something very specific to the human nervous system. It teaches us, slowly and thoroughly, that we are powerless. That what happens out there is too vast, too entrenched, too irreversible for anything we could do in here to matter. And so the nervous system, in its profound wisdom, does what it always does with unbearable helplessness: it shuts down. Goes numb. Finds a way to create distance from the thing that hurts too much to face.

And in spiritual communities, that shutdown wears spiritual clothes. It calls itself holding space. Maintaining frequency. Trusting the divine plan. Surrendering to what is.

I am not dismissing any of these practices. I am asking us to be honest about when they serve our evolution and when they serve our avoidance.

Our Emotions Are Not Problems to Regulate Away

Our grief about what is happening in the world is not just a wound that needs healing. Our rage at what is being done to the innocent is not just a trauma response to regulate. Our heartbreak, when we witness what is happening to children, to women, to the earth, to the most vulnerable, is not a dysregulation to be soothed back into calm.

It is our humanity speaking. It is the part of us that has not forgotten that we are not separate from each other. That love without action is just a feeling we have in the privacy of our own hearts. That consciousness without responsibility is just a very beautiful prison.

Our feelings are not the problem. What we do with them, that is the question.

The most painful thing I witness is how we hold space for everyone else's pain while quietly keeping our own power at a safe distance. Somewhere underneath all the spiritual maturity, there is still a part of us that is afraid of what happens when we take up the full space we were born to occupy.

The Leadership Awakening

We have been trained into smallness by a world that was built by people who needed us small. And reclaiming our full power, our full voice, our full presence, our full willingness to lead, is not just a personal development achievement. It is an act of profound resistance against everything that has tried to keep us quiet and manageable.

This moment in history is asking for leadership. Not the kind that has all the answers, that is immune to grief, that has transcended its own fear and confusion. The kind that is heartbroken and still building. That feels the full weight of what is happening and lets that weight make it more committed, not less. That does not rise above its humanity but goes so deeply into it that it forges something real.

We have had the spiritual awakening. Many of us have spent years in the opening, the expansion, the dissolution of old identity. All of that was necessary. All of it was real.

Now this is the leadership awakening. The part where everything we woke up to must come into form. Must become action. Must become something the world can actually feel and be changed by, not just something we carry beautifully in private.

May our collective trauma not be the thing that shrinks us, but the thing that finally makes us too awake and too fiercely loving to stay silent and small any longer.

The world is waiting on us. Not on the version of us that is perfectly regulated and spiritually polished. The real version. The one that is scared and showing up anyway. The one that is grieving and building anyway. The one that feels everything, and has decided to let it move her forward.

With Love,
Madalena Dragon
