Enlightenment or Dissociation?

There is a very thin line between spiritual expansion and emotional avoidance. One takes you deeper into yourself. The other takes you away from yourself while looking spiritual the entire time.

Madalena Dragon Spirituality 6 min read

The spiritual path is one of the most profound things a human being can walk. It is also one of the easiest paths to use as an escape.

Not because spirituality is wrong. But because the language of spirituality, the vocabulary of detachment, surrender, non-resistance, higher self, can be used to dress up avoidance so beautifully that you cannot tell the difference.

I couldn't tell the difference for years.

And I work with women who can't tell the difference right now. Women who are deeply devoted to their practice, who meditate, who journal, who go on retreats, and who are somehow still not fully in their lives.

Still not in their bodies. Still not feeling their feelings. Still not making the moves their souls are calling them toward.

Peaceful on the surface. Disconnected underneath.

What Real Enlightenment Feels Like

Genuine spiritual expansion makes you more present, not less. It makes you more embodied, not more detached. It makes you more capable of feeling, not more protected from feeling.

When you genuinely grow spiritually, you become more available to your life. More available to grief, to joy, to desire, to anger, to love. All of it. Because you have developed the capacity to hold it.

Real awakening does not make you float above your life. It drops you deeper into it.

What Dissociation Looks Like When It Wears Spiritual Clothes

Here is what spiritually-dressed dissociation looks like. You might recognise yourself here. That is okay. I recognise myself here too.

  • You use "surrender" to avoid making a decision that terrifies you
  • You use "detachment" to stop yourself from wanting things you're afraid you won't get
  • You use "everything happens for a reason" to skip grief
  • You use "I'm not attached to outcomes" to protect yourself from caring
  • You use "I need to heal more before I'm ready" as a reason to never fully show up
  • You use "trust the universe" as a substitute for taking action
  • You use "high vibe only" to bypass anger, sadness, or fear
  • You use "I'm very spiritual" as a subtle way to feel superior and avoid intimacy

None of these things are wrong in themselves. Surrender is real. Detachment is real. Trust is real.

But there is a version of each of them that is about expansion, and a version that is about protection.

Only you know which one you're in.

The Test

Here is how you tell the difference.

Ask yourself: does this spiritual practice or belief bring me closer to my life, or further from it?

Does it make me more present in my body, or less? Does it increase my capacity to feel, or decrease it? Does it move me toward the things I actually want, or does it give me a reason to stay exactly where I am?

Expansion is uncomfortable. It asks you to feel things, to risk things, to be seen, to step into places where you cannot control the outcome.

Dissociation is comfortable. It has a spiritual framework that makes staying still feel like wisdom.

The Most Dangerous Kind

The most dangerous form of spiritual dissociation is the kind that looks the most devoted.

The woman who meditates two hours a day but has not cried in three years. The woman who speaks fluently about frequency and vibration but cannot feel her own body. The woman who has done every healing modality available and still has not started the work she knows she is here to do.

Spiritual practice is not the problem. The intention behind it is the question.

Are you going inward to meet yourself, or to escape yourself?

How to Come Back

If you recognise yourself here, the way back is not to abandon your practice. It is to bring your body back in.

Feel the chair beneath you right now. Feel your feet on the floor. Notice if there is something you have been calling "surrender" that is actually fear of disappointment. Notice if there is something you have been calling "non-attachment" that is actually protection from wanting.

Real spirituality is not the absence of desire. It is the willingness to desire fully, to feel fully, to be human fully, while knowing that you are held.

That is the most advanced spiritual practice there is.

Not transcending your humanity. Descending into it, without losing yourself.
