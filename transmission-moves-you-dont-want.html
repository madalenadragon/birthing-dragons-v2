The Money Is on the Other Side of the Moves You Don't Want to Make

It is not that you don't know what to do. You know exactly what the move is. You have known for months. Maybe longer.

Let me be direct with you, because I think you are ready for it.

The problem is not information. You have consumed enough content, done enough courses, attended enough masterclasses. You know what the move is. You have probably known for a long time. The strategy is not the gap. The clarity is not actually missing. What is missing is the willingness to make the move that you already know needs to be made.

And that willingness is not missing because you are weak or undisciplined or not ready. It is missing because the move asks something of you that you have not yet decided you are willing to give.

What the Move Actually Asks

The move exposes you. It requires you to be visible in a way you have been avoiding, to step out of the background where it has been safe to build quietly and into a more fully seen version of yourself that cannot be taken back.

The move requires a decision. A real one. Not the kind you can hedge or qualify or revisit indefinitely. And a real decision means you can no longer blame the circumstances, the timing, the market, the algorithm. Once you decide and act, the responsibility is yours. That is terrifying for anyone who has learned to use ambiguity as a shield.

The move is irreversible. You cannot un-become who you are becoming. You cannot unknow what you will learn. You cannot un-be seen once you are seen. And some part of you knows that, and is using all its cleverness to delay the moment when the old version of you has to finally step aside.

The problem has never been information. The problem is that the move asks something of you that you are not sure you are ready to give.

The Discomfort Is Not a Warning

Here is what I want you to understand: the discomfort you feel when you think about making this move is not a signal to back down. It is not your intuition saying no. It is not misalignment or bad timing or a sign from the universe to wait.

It is a sign that you are expanding. That you are doing something real. Something that actually matters, to you, to your mission, to the people waiting for what you have to offer.

Everyone who built real impact and real income crossed a moment where the move felt impossible. Where the ask felt too large, the risk too high, the visibility too exposing. And they made it anyway. Not because they felt ready. Because they decided the vision mattered more than the fear.

That moment, the one you are standing in right now, is not an obstacle. It is the portal.

What Is on the Other Side

On the other side of the move you keep not making is the income you know is available to you. The clients who are genuinely aligned. The business that feels like an expression of who you actually are rather than a performance of who you think you should be. The version of yourself that you have been practising in private, calling not yet ready, keeping just out of reach.

None of that arrives before the move. All of it arrives because of it.

You do not get the result and then make the move. You make the move and then the result becomes possible. The confidence does not come first. The momentum does not come first. The proof that it will work does not come first. The move comes first, and everything else is built on top of it.

The discomfort is not a sign to back down. It is a sign you are expanding. That moment you keep avoiding? That is the portal.

What to Do Right Now

You already know what the move is. So I am not going to tell you to get more clarity, do more planning, or wait until you feel more aligned. I am going to ask you something simpler and far more difficult:

What would happen if you made the move today? Not perfectly. Not when all the conditions are right. Not after one more round of preparation. Today, with what you have, as you are.

The version of you who has already built what you are building didn't wait until she was ready. She decided that what she was building mattered more than the story about why she couldn't yet. She made the move uncomfortable and she made it anyway.

That version of you is not far away. She is one decision away. She always has been.

With Love,
Madalena Dragon
